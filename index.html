The Client

Roger Neilson House is named after the NHL coach Roger Neilson, a passionate advocate for children and recipient of the Order of Canada. It is a collaborative project of the Ottawa Senators Hockey Club, the Ottawa Senators Foundation, and the Childrenâ€™s Hospital of Eastern Ontario (CHEO). The house fulfills the dream of providing pediatric palliative care services, catering to the needs of both the child and the family, ensuring timely access to essential services.

The Conflict

The current philanthropic market is struggling with user engagment and proof of legitimacy which leads to a decline in donations and volunteers.

The Problem:

Time is valuable for users, if they percieve the application will be a long process, it is less likely that they will complete the application process. 90% of our users stated they would not complete the volunteer application process if they are required to input information more than once.

We pondered How might we build trust and confidence with our potential user? We plan on measuring success with the following Key Preformance Indicators: Increase in volunteer applications & Time spent on task.

Prototype & Test

Sketches

To prioritize users' time, we have strategically placed the mission statement above the fold on the landing page. This eliminates the need for users to navigate to the "About" page or scroll for crucial information.

Our usability study revealed that users enjoyed the concept of "What you'll need" before the application process. This element was present in the original design so we kept it and made minor tweeks.

The initial volunteer pathway elicited a sense of overwhelm among users due to the excessive amount of text. Additionally, there are inaccuracies in the steps, with step three being duplicated. These errors may cause users to hesitate and question the legitimacy of the process. As we've recognized, ensuring user confidence in the legitimacy of the pathway is crucial.

UI Style Guide

Conclusion:

Our findings indicated that 60% of the participants preferred having the 'About Us' section included in the primary navigation bar rather than the secondary navigation links, as originally designed. In response to this valuable feedback, we made the necessary changes and incorporated the About section into the primary navigation bar. Another significant insight we gained from the study is the crucial importance of users being aware that they are on an official not-for-profit webpage. This is particularly crucial for volunteers and donors, as it builds trust and encourages their involvement. To achieve this, easy access to the About Us section was identified as a vital step in establishing the organization's credibility and reliability.
